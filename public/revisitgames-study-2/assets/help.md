In this study, you will be randomly assigned to the **having-navigation-system group** or the **NOT-having-navigation-system group**. No matter which group you will be assigned to, you will go through 2 sessions: (1) the training session and (2) the testing session. 

 (1) For the **training session**, you will play a small video game demo with two different maps (with or without navigation systems), in which you will be asked to navigate and move to 8 destinations of each map (16 in total) in a digital city environment. 

 (2) For the **testing session**, you will firstly be asked to answer several questions about the locations of the map, and then replay these two maps of the game, and navigate the 4 destinations (8 in total) of each map without having any navigation systems.

---

Whenever you finish a game map (no matter if you successfully navigated to the destinations, or not), you will see a tip of **"This is the end of the game. Please return to the survey."** on your screen as shown in below:

You will also be able to revisit this gameplay information at any time by clicking the **Help** button in the top-right corner of the screen. We suggest maximizing your browser window, as the video game can be on the larger side.

<img src="./assets/instruction_01.png" width="auto" height="300px">

In the city, there is a Goal Location. Find it before time runs out. At the end of each round, the Goal Location will switch to a different location. If you run out of time in a round before reaching the Goal Location, go to the next one.

## Game Control:

**W**, **A**, **S**, **D** or **↑**, **↓**, **←**, **→**: Movement.

**Space** key: Jump.

**M** key: Look at the map.

**Mouse**: Rotate the view.

---

# For the Having-Strong-Navigation-System Group:

You have an always-on mini-map in the top right corner of the screen, and an additional hint of the path to the destination with a solid yellow line permanently drawn in the ground. You also have access to a full-screen pop-up map (invoked with the **M** key) that shows the target destination, your location, and the path hint.
