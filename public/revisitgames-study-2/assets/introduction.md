# Evaluating Player Experience in the Navigation System of a 3D Game

Thank you for participating in this research study! 

This study aims to evaluate your playing experiences in terms of the impact of having (or not having) navigation systems (such as mini-map, map menu, or route-tip). 

You will be randomly assigned to the **having-navigation-system** group or the **NOT-having-navigation-system** group. No matter which group you will be assigned to, you will first play a small video game demo with two different maps as a **training session** (with or without navigation systems), in which you will be asked to navigate and move to several destinations in a digital city environment. After the training session, in the **testing session**, you will firstly be asked to answer several questions about the locations of the map, and then replay these two maps of the game, and navigate the destination without having any navigation systems.

The study has three sections: (1) The first will show you explanations of the study objectives and procedures, as well as a digital consent form; (2) the second section includes playing the small video game (including the training session and the testing session); and (3) after finishing the game, the last section will show you a follow-up questionnaire that asks about your playing experiences.
